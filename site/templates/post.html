Japanese Euphonic Changes

MAR 13, 2025


Rendaku (連濁) and sokuonbin (促音便) are the two most famous 音便 (おんびん, euphonic sound change) patterns in Japanese. Furthermore they’re part of a larger family of historical and modern sound changes that make speech flow more naturally. Let’s go over the main types:

1. 連濁 (Rendaku) — Sequential Voicing

The first consonant of the second word in a compound often becomes voiced.
Example:
- 手 (て) + 紙 (かみ) → 手紙（てがみ）
- 山 (やま) + 川 (かわ) → 山川（やまがわ）
Not universal: some words resist it.
The “Lyman’s Law” rule prevents rendaku if the second word already has a voiced consonant later in it.

2. 促音便 (Sokuonbin) — Gemination / “small っ”

A consonant doubles, often replacing an old tsu-like sound in classical Japanese or to smooth the rhythm.
Example:
- 六個（ろっこ） instead of ろくこ
- 一回（いっかい） instead of いちかい
- 学校（がっこう） from がく + こう

3. 撥音便 (Hatsuonbin) — Nasal Assimilation / “ん” Change

A final m, n, ng sound (ん) absorbs or alters the following consonant.
Example:
- よみて → よんで (読んで)
- しにて → しんで (死んで)
- かみて → かんで (噛んで)
This one is why te-forms of verbs with mi, ni, bi lines turn into んで.

4. イ音便 (I-onbin) — “i” Glide

A k, g, h, b, m ending becomes an i-like sound for smoother flow.
Example:
- 書く → 書いて (かく → かいて)
- 泳ぐ → 泳いで (およぐ → およいで)
- 話す → 話して (はなす → はなして)

5. ウ音便 (U-onbin) — “u” Glide

A sound changes to end in u for smoothness, often with older verbs.
Example:
- 問ふ → 問う (old → modern)
- 思ふ → 思う

6. 長音化 (Chōonka) — Vowel Lengthening

A vowel is extended to make the rhythm more natural or to distinguish meaning.
Example:
- おねえさん (older sister) from おねえ + さん
- おばあさん (grandmother)
- とおい (far) → lengthened “o”

7. 音の脱落 (Elision / Contraction)

Some sounds drop out in rapid or casual speech.
Example:
- ～ている → ～てる
- ～ておく → ～とく
- ～なければ → ～なきゃ

8. 音の挿入 (Epenthesis)

A vowel or sound is inserted for ease of pronunciation.
Example:
- クラス + メート → クラスメート (the vowel “u” helps the flow)
- The added “i” or “u” sounds when adapting foreign words:
  - Strike → ストライク
  - Club → クラブ
